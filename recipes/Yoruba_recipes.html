Ekuru

Description

Ekuru is a Nigerian steamed bean pudding made from peeled beans, popularly eaten with a spicy stew or sauce. It's a lighter, milder cousin of moin moin, often enjoyed as a breakfast dish in the southwestern part of Nigeria.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups peeled brown or black-eyed beans
  • onions
  • dried and or fresh pepper
  • groundnut oil
  • palm oil
  • salt and maggi to taste
  • Iru
  • fish and meat (optional)
  • Water

Steps

  1. Soak the beans for few hours, and then peel of the skin
  2. Blend the peeled beans with onion, adding just enough water to get a thick, smooth paste
  3. Season the paste with salt (optional)
  4. add 4 tea spoon of groundnut oil to the paste
  5. turn the paste with turning stick until it becomes smooth and softer
  6. Wrap the paste in leaves or nylon or steaming mold
  7. Steam for 45-60 minutes until firm
  8. blend your pepper and onion (add tomatoes if you don't want it too spicy)
  9. in your frying pan, pour your red oil and add some chopped onion.
  10. stir the onion and allow it to fry for two minutes
  11. add your peper mixture and stir together
  12. add your salt, magie and Iru
  13. add your fish and meat
  14. Allow it to fry very well
  15. serve your food hot
